### 3b. Layout guard false-positive on dark pages — SCRIPT FIXED
Pages **43** and **61** were skipped with `suspicious Mag column width`. Cause: these are
darker scans whose vertical rules print bold/bleed wide (44–53 px vs the normal 7–8 px),

the guard now validates the **crop width** (`40 ≤ width(crops[2]) ≤ 200`), i.e. the region
actually sent to the model, not the white gap. Both pages re-extracted cleanly afterward.

### 3d. The even-page N°-column miss — `detect_layout` FIXED (2026-06-21)
Every garbage-number page above (144, 158, 304, 368, 410) is an **even/verso** page. On even
pages the printed table is shifted right (recto/verso margin alternation), and the page's
trimmed/binding edge leaves a faint vertical rule within a few px of the image border. In
`detect_layout` the border-pair vote (two heavy rules 1900–2090 px apart enclosing the most
inner rules) then **tied** between the true pair (e.g. p410 mid 328 ↔ 2356) and a wrong pair
anchored on that edge rule (mid 448 ↔ 2506); because the loop scans right-to-left with a strict
`>`, the wrong, N°-skipping pair won. Result: col 0 landed on the constellation column and every
column shifted, so the model read dates/constellations as "numbers" → the digit-drop garbage.

**Fix:** drop rule groups within 25 px of either image edge before the border vote
(`groups = [g for g in groups if g[0] > 25 and g[1] < w-25]`). Real table borders are always
well inside the page, so no legitimate rule is removed. Verified: cols[0] now correct on
144/158/304/368/410; **unchanged** on good pages (213/328/408/411/564); full-page regression
sweep clean. This fixes the column detection for BOTH the Gemini pipeline and the subagent.
Page **410** was then re-scanned with the subagent (56 rows 21287–21342, 54/56 refs, all mags ==
gc.txt, ground-truth 21287 B.5438 / 21288 B.5435 / 21341 blank-"neb." / 21342 ZC.2641 confirmed).

### 5d. Reference validation of magnitude-flagged rows
Rationale: when Gemini **misread the magnitude** of a row (a §2a CORRECTED entry), it may
have struggled with that whole row, so its **reference** is also suspect → verified against
the print. Rows where the print simply differs from gc.txt (§2b KEPT) need no such check —

(num+mag+ref); a raw crop spanning far-apart columns misaligns because the print rows lean
up to half a row across the page (the row anchor is the **magnitude**, not the number).

### 13b. Interleaved stacked fractions
Fraction numerators print on the star's own row but the denominator descends into
the next row's zone, so consecutive fractional rows form a single vertical glyph
chain. Read by **enumerating glyphs top→bottom (2 per fraction) and assigning
num/den pairs by row**; e.g. p36 HEIS rows 697–700 chain `1,3|1,3|2,3|1,3` →
5⅓, 5⅓, 5⅔, 6⅓. Never trust a fraction denominator below ~4× zoom: first-pass
misreads corrected this way include 674 Lal 6¼ (guessed ¾), 690 Lal 5¾ (guessed ½),
692 Lal 7¾ (guessed ½), 671 ARGEL 5⅔ (guessed plain 5).

The log emits 251 enumerated warnings in four classes. Classes 1 (near star with

* **Class 4 — star "FAR" from its counterpart**: **14 entries.**

## 19. Method

For each warning the printed row (and its whole decade block) was
rendered at 200-400 DPI. The decisive test is: does the flagged digital value
**equal the printed value** (→ the discrepancy was introduced by Gilliss and merely
copied faithfully = printed-catalogue error = *false positive*) or does it **differ
from the printed value** (→ digitization typo = *requires correction*)? Physics
(the recomputed precession) supplies the correct sign/magnitude and, together with
the neighbouring rows and any duplicate-observation link, adjudicates ambiguous
cases. Minus signs in the scan are frequently faint, so a missing "−" was never
read as positive on its own — the physics sign was used as the arbiter.

## 20. Result

Of the 156 audited entries, **143 are false positives** (the printed register
agrees with the digital one; the error, where any, is Gilliss's own and was copied
faithfully) and **13 diverge from the printed catalogue.** Nine of the thirteen are
genuine digitization typos in a **precession or cross-identification** field and
were corrected in *cat/gilliss.txt*; the other four turned out **not** to be
digitization typos at all (see the next subsection) and were left unchanged.

**Applied — nine corrections written to *cat/gilliss.txt* (positions untouched):**

### The four that were *not* changed — the digital position was already correct

The remaining four warnings (e14 G 1045, e57 G 3432, e78 G 4316, e111 G 5772)
originally looked like position typos, because the digital **position** disagrees
with the printed row while the digital **precession** matches it. Writing the
printed position into the file, however, made *cross_south* flag each of the four as
**"ALONE"** (no PPM / CD / CPD / GSC counterpart) — whereas at their *original*
digital positions all four are matched to a real reference star. The digital
positions are further corroborated by the stars' duplicate-observation twins:

| Star | Digital pos (kept) | Printed pos | Corroborated by |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| G 1045 | −79°16′08.4″ | −76°16′08.4″ | twin G 1044 = −79°16′07.9″, pRA −0.224 (physics-consistent) |
| G 3432 | RA 5ʰ40ᵐ27.10ˢ | RA 5ʰ44ᵐ27.10ˢ | twin G 3381 = 5ʰ40ᵐ27.17ˢ, pDe +1.71 (physics-consistent) |
| G 4316 | RA 6ʰ48ᵐ49.04ˢ | RA 6ʰ43ᵐ49.04ˢ | twin G 4374 = 6ʰ48ᵐ48.95ˢ, pDe −4.24 (physics-consistent) |
| G 5772 | RA 8ʰ41ᵐ59.85ˢ | RA 8ʰ43ᵐ59.85ˢ | matched a reference star at the digital position (no twin) |

The correct reading of these four is therefore the reverse of a digitization typo:
**Gilliss's printed position is wrong** (his two observations of the same star
disagree — e.g. G 3432 at 5ʰ44ᵐ vs. its twin G 3381 at 5ʰ40ᵐ), and the digitiser
had **already corrected the position** to the true sky value. What is stale in the
digital file is the *precession* field, which still matches Gilliss's uncorrected
position — and that mismatch is exactly what the precession check reports. Forcing
the position back to the printed value would re-inject Gilliss's error and orphan
the star, so per instruction these four are **left unchanged**. If internal
consistency is later wanted without moving the star, the fix is to update the
*precession* to the physics value at the (correct) digital position: G 1045
pRA → −0.224, G 3432 pDe → +1.71, G 4316 pDe → −4.24, G 5772 pRA → +0.191.

**Likely origin of the four (working hypothesis).** The most economical explanation
is a **corrigenda / errata sheet issued after the printed catalogue was published**:
such an errata would have re-observed or re-reduced these stars and given the
**corrected positions**, and whoever built the digital file applied those corrected
positions — but left the **annual-precession** columns untouched (the errata
presumably listed only the position, and the precession was never recomputed to
match). This accounts for the whole pattern at once: the digital position lands on
the right star (matches a reference catalogue and, where present, the star's
duplicate twin), the printed position is Gilliss's original wrong value, and the
precession still corresponds to that superseded printed position — which is exactly
what makes the precession check fire. It also explains why these four differ in
character from the nine genuine keystroke typos above (sign flips, dropped digits,
a shifted cross-id), which touch a single precession/identifier field and leave the
position alone. Under this reading the digital file is *more* correct than the
printed catalogue for these stars, and the only outstanding work is to bring the
precession columns into line with the corrected positions (values listed above).

### Notes on the nine applied correction types

* **Sign errors (e64, e82, e104, e113, e39).** The precession magnitude and the
  position match the printed row; only the sign of the RA precession is wrong in
  the digital file. In each the recomputed precession confirms the printed sign
  (e.g. e104 α Chamaeleontis: printed −1.438, physics −1.434, digital +1.438). e39
  additionally has a wrong last digit (printed +0.018, digital −0.015).
* **Value miscopies (e19, e216).** The digital number simply differs from the
  printed one, which itself agrees with the physics (e19 pRA −6.271 for −5.329;
  e216 pDe +3.08 for +3.34).
* **Digit dropout (e174).** Printed 15.335 lost its leading "1" → digital 5.335.
* **Mis-assigned cross-identification (e227, class 4).** Star 14702's position
  matches printed row 14702 (**Gould 28204**) exactly, but the digital file tagged it

### Why the false positives are false positives

* **Class 3 (130 of 142).** In every case the digital precession equals the printed
  precession while the physics disagrees, i.e. Gilliss's tabulated precession is

  blocks are systematic: the pDe of printed rows 1426-1429 reads ~15 where physics

  printed pRA off by 0.03-0.10 s. In a handful (e17 G 1142, e33 G 1767) the printed
  **sign** is wrong but the digital already carries the correct sign, so no change
  is needed.
* **Class 4 (13 of 14).** The digital position matches the printed row exactly; the
  large separation is a cross-identification artefact — "½"-designation Gould stars
  with no clean GC counterpart (e40/e41, ~60°), plain wrong GC-number matches
  (e92/e103, ~35-42°), or a genuine Gilliss-vs-reference position difference for the
  same star (e51 GC 6499 0.9°; e233 Lacaille 8696 0.3°, digital cross-id correct).
